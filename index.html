About me

I am a PhD student at Nara Institute of Science and Technology (Social Computing Lab), Japan.
My research interests include medical natural language processing, health informatics, information extraction, machine learning, and deep learning.
My current research is to automate meta-analysis by using natural language processing methods. Meta-analysis is the process of collecting and analyzing previous studies to ascertain if a treatment is effective or not. Meta-analysis provide the gold standard for medical evidence. Despite their importance, meta-analyses are time- and cost-consuming and this is challenging especially in cases where timeliness is important. In my research, I am developing a system for automating meta-analysis, especially the data extraction and statistical analysis steps.
